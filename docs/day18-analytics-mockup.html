1. How an admin gets there

Analytics is a top-level admin tab — same row as Members, Events, Announcements. One click from anywhere in the app.

Non-admin members never see the link (the entire /admin/* tree is gated by the existing requireAdmin() guard).

3. Interactions on each card

Cards are intentionally low-interaction. The admin's job here is to read numbers and form a hypothesis, not to drill in. That keeps Day 18 small.

What you can do

  • Hover a card → native browser tooltip explains what the number means (the title attribute).
  • Glance at the sub-line → see the underlying counts ("12 of 14 within 7 days") so the percentage isn't context-free.
  • Refresh the page → numbers re-query. There's no auto-refresh polling.

What you can't do (yet)

  • Click a card to drill into the underlying rows.
  • Change the date range.
  • Export to CSV.
  • See a sparkline / trend chart.
  • Filter by tag, year, or graduation cohort.

5. The 6 cards in detail

Card What it answers Backing view
Invite → Active Of people invited in the last 30 days, what % made it to "active" (signed up + approved + has profile)? analytics_invited_to_completed
Active members How many distinct members signed in at least once in the last 30 days? analytics_active_members
Mentorship request rate Are members actually using the mentorship feature? Requests-per-active-member per 30 days. analytics_mentorship_request_rate
Mentor response rate When mentees reach out, do mentors reply within 7 days? Quality signal for the network. analytics_mentorship_response_rate
Profile freshness What % of profiles have been updated in the last 6 months? Decays naturally over time. analytics_profile_freshness
RSVP activity Across all upcoming events, how many seats are committed (going + waitlist)? analytics_rsvp_activity

6. What we're not building (yet)

Explicitly deferred from Day 18. Each of these is a real feature, but cutting them keeps this PR small and demo-ready.
  • Sparklines / trend charts — would need a charting lib (Recharts or similar). Adds bundle weight + design work.
  • Custom date ranges — needs a date-picker UI and a parameterized query path.
  • Drilldown on click — "show me the 12 mentors who responded" is valuable but a separate UI surface.
  • CSV export — could ride along, but it's an admin-power-tool concern (PR C) not a metrics concern.
  • Click-tracking events table — separate concern. The 6 cards above are computed from existing tables; we don't need a new event log to ship this.
  • Per-cohort filters ("show me only Class of 2010") — interesting for board narratives but easy to add later.
  • Email digest — weekly summary mailed to admins. Post-demo.
