  5. Your open asks — status pills in the vocabulary the redesign settled: 2 offers / Waiting / 1 declined / Closes in 3d, header carrying the ambient 3 of 5 slot count. Row → the ask's status view.
  6. From the school (rail) — next 2 events (date tile, Going chip, → event page) + newest/pinned announcement (→ reading page). No newsletter here — School's card owns it. Everything clicks straight through.
  7. Profile freshness (rail) — appears only when enrichment has something to approve; one card → the owner-only review queue. Absent otherwise — never a standing chore.
What Home deliberately doesn't have

No activity feed or stream (the loss condition in miniature) · no stats or "you've helped N people" (the same scoreboard rule as profiles) · no "members you may know" carousel (that's People's job — Home shows people only when attached to an action) · no notifications list (the bell exists; duplicating it makes both untrustworthy) · no lingering onboarding checklist · no second search box (the ask entry is need-shaped, and per-surface search lives on People and Help; there's no global palette). Home earns trust by holding only what's true and actionable this week.

Why the rotating spotlight isn't a feed: it auto-advances — the one motion we allow — but it's fenced so the motion serves glancing, not capture: it pauses on hover/focus (so an action never slides out from under you), honors reduced-motion, is manually steerable, capped at ~6 items, ranked by nothing, and every "More →" exits to a real page instead of scrolling deeper. A feed optimizes time-on-page; the spotlight optimizes time-to-action. Any tag that stops producing offers, asks, or clicks comes out.

The five-slot rule Decided 2026-07-06

Five open asks at once — direct or circle; the sixth waits. Slots return when an ask ends: resolved, retracted, declined by every recipient, or closed at day 14. On desktop the count is ambient — Home's module and the status rail show 3 of 5; it never interrupts.

Buffer check, asker side

The asker sees who could answer, and why, before writing a word · search results are private — a member never learns they appeared and weren't asked · a no arrives as one kind, cushioned note, never silence and never a bare rejection · no read states, no timelines · the asker holds the accept on every offer · and the private tier is the open-ask default. Asking never costs face.

1Offers open this same dialog, with a suggested note · 4silence: one day-5 nudge, then the quiet day-14 close
  1. Offer, with a note that writes itself Decided 2026-07-06 — a default line or your own, AI-helped either way. Sent state is calm; withdrawing while pending is quiet.
  2. Accept — with a first word. Default opening message or custom, AI-helped; the thread never starts cold. Origin line on the asker's side: "Maya accepted your ask."
  3. Decline — always with a note, three ways Decided 2026-07-06 · supersedes ADR 0011 D5. Escalating cushion: a default reason · write your own (AI polishes) · or let the AI handle it — tell it casually what's going on and it composes & sends the kind note for you, so you never address the asker in your own voice. The item leaves your list; the note reaches the asker in real time; a decline resets the strike count.
  4. Silence still resolves itself. Day 5: one gentle reminder. Day 14: the pending item disappears — no guilt screen — and the timeout counts one of three strikes toward auto-pause.
Buffer check, helper side

Availability is a mood, not a contract — the switch is the only control, no clock ever expires it, and only leaving three asks unanswered pauses it (quietly, reversibly) · declining is two taps — pick a kind default reason or let AI shape your own — so saying no never means writing a rejection · silence still resolves itself at day 14, no guilt screen · offers that aren't taken up close no-fault · and there is no meter, streak, or slot count anywhere on this page. Saying no never costs face — and the asker is never left guessing.

The brand mechanism, audited

Two-sided buffer — what each side sees, moment by moment

The rule: every decline is cushioned and symmetric — default reasons and AI help on both sides, so saying no never means drafting a rejection and hearing no never means silence. Every send path shows the sender exactly who will see it; every waiting state is honest but calm. Any screen that violates this table is wrong regardless of how it looks.

Decision record

Decided in review — Richard, 2026-07-05/06

The record the screens get designed against. The two seams at the end are the only things left to watch in the pilot.

Decided
Reach is immutable once published

No edits mid-flight. Retract anytime (everything vanishes quietly, the slot frees) or let the 14-day close run. Widening = the post-close "Open it to the circle" — a new ask, not an edit.

Decided
Five open asks at once

Total cap, not a recipient limit. Slots return when an ask ends — resolved, retracted, declined by every recipient, or closed at day 14.

Decided · reversed 07-06
One recipient per ask

No multi-select. To ask several people, send each their own tailored ask from the results — every note stays genuinely 1:1, and the five-slot cap prevents spam.

Decided
Anonymity, per open ask, in v1

Anonymous or named at post time. Anonymous = revealed only to the accepted helper; evidence lines derive from the ask's text only.

Decided
No quiet pass — every decline carries a note

Both directions: default reasons ("I can't take this on right now", "This one's outside what I can speak to"), custom, or AI-shaped. Supersedes ADR 0011 · D5 (ADR amended 2026-07-06).

Decided
Give status: no clock, three strikes

"Open to helping" never expires on a timer. Three timed-out direct asks → quiet auto-pause. Any response, accept or decline, resets the count.

Decided
Topics: five, editable

Up to 5 standing topic chips on Give, add/remove anytime. They drive the suggestion arm and Home's "Needs you this week."

Decided
Messages everywhere have a default

Offer notes, accept openers, and decline notes: default line or custom, AI-helped either way.

Decided
Notification popover ships v1

Ask received, offer received, accepted, decline notes — each shown once, deep-linking; then they live only in notifications and Messages.

Decided
Smaller calls

Outcome note on resolve: yes, optional + skippable. Default Help mode: remember last, Get for first-timers. Weekly digest: not yet. Global search / ⌘K: removed entirely — search lives per-surface (People, Help). Messages: "Waiting on you" is a foldable pinned group with a red count badge; the context rail folds/expands within the chat; no Schedule a call button.

Decided
School: glance cover + event page

The navy cover holds the quick-glance essentials (description line, host, When/Where/Format, RSVP); a separate event page holds itinerary, parking, cost, host contact, who's-going. Attending strip stays as the one my-RSVPs surface ("Manage →" dropped). Capacity fill bar → calm text + waitlist. "Viewing" chip → selection styling. Newsletter → light card. Times member-local first, campus second.

Decided · clarified 07-06
People & Help — no cross-routing

People always returns people; Help always asks. The only variation is invisible backend — keyword search vs the NL/vector pipeline, auto-picked, same result shape + explanation either way. No "sounds like an ask?" nudge, no name-jump. Each surface owns its lane.

Decided 07-06
Scope & membership

One organization to start (multi-school later possible). Members = alumni + students within 4 years of graduating — middle-school and younger can't join.

Decided 07-06
Decline gains an AI-relay layer

Three ways, escalating cushion: default reason · write-your-own (AI polishes) · let the AI handle it — tell it casually ("slammed this month") and it composes & sends the kind note, so you never address the asker in your own voice.

Decided 07-06
14-day clock → a late warning

No running "Day N of 14" countdown anywhere (against calm-not-urgent). A quiet "3 days left before this ask closes" flag appears only in the final 3 days — status view and the thread rail.

Decided 07-06
Trust & safety

Report (→ admin moderation pipeline, private), Block (mutual-invisible; anonymity doesn't defeat it), Disconnect (leave a circle quietly, keep history). Plus the cold-start hand-off, notifications section, settings, ask-history, and my-circle surfaces — all mapped.
