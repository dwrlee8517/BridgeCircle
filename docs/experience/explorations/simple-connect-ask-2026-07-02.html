Exploration · simplified model — supersedes the 5-type exploration

Two verbs, one inbox

A member can do exactly two things to another member: Connect or Ask. If you don't know who to ask, ask the circle. Every accepted intro becomes an ordinary conversation in Messages — no ask threads, no mentorship screens, no relationship types. The AI's whole job is the first message; after that, the product gets out of the way.

The model

Three front doors, one gate, one room

Strangers can't just message each other. Every path in goes through one first message and one accept — then it's just Messages. The three doors differ only in who you're approaching and why; what's behind them is identical.

What this deletes: ask types (advice/mentorship), commitment tiers and pace cards, screening steps, peer lanes, scoped-thread chrome, per-function interfaces. What stays, invisibly: the accept gate, the quiet pass, and AI matching for open asks.

Door 2 · connect

Connecting — a quick hello, or an AI-shaped intro

Connect is for the relationship, not a request. Two ways in: already know each other? One tap on a quick hello and it's sent. Reaching someone new? Tell the AI why, like you'd tell ChatGPT, and it shapes a proper intro. Either way: mutual accept, then a plain conversation.

Grounding. Simplified per Richard's direction of 2026-07-02: two primitives (Connect / Ask), open asks to the circle, AI-written first messages, and all conversations converging in Messages with no per-function interfaces. This intentionally supersedes the visible commitment-tier axis, pace cards, screening steps, and peer lanes from ADR 0010 D1 and the earlier exploration (help-relationship-types-2026-07-02.html, kept for comparison). Preserved from the existing product: the quiet pass and never-surfaced declines (PRs #106–107), AI draft disclosure and message style (voice-guidelines §10), and the ADR 0003 gate shapes (connect two-sided, ask one-sided) — now as invisible plumbing rather than interface. Colors and type per Civic Editorial tokens.md: amber for the asker's social commitment, sage for offering help, blue for mutual connection. All members fictional.
