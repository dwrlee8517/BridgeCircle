How BridgeCircle is used — the member side and the admin side

The main ways people use the app, drawn as one flywheel rather than three features. Amber is an asker's moment of commitment; sage is a helper offering. Roles are never fixed.

A school's warm network is real but inert — the data rots, the alumni drift, and even when the right person is right there, asking feels too awkward to do. BridgeCircle keeps the circle alive and makes asking and helping effortless on both sides.

How a member uses it

demand & supply are the same person

Five jobs. Asking and offering are two faces of one object, and the same person does both — a senior can ask a junior; a peer can answer a peer. Nothing brands anyone a "mentor."

The whole point: no one is "the mentor"

The same member is an asker in one thread and a helper in another — and the direction can flip inside a single thread. "Mentorship" isn't a role here; it's just the name for help that happens to be ongoing.

How conversations connect

one thread · two openers · optional connection

Every conversation is one thread. You reach it two ways — an ask or a hello — and accepting an ask opens the thread but does not auto-connect you. Connection is a separate, mutual "let's keep this" you can add later.

Ask for help

One-sided · passable · matchable · can stay open. The helper accepts, or passes quietly.

Connect — a hello

Mutual · guided. They accept → you're connected.

accept · (ask: or pass)

One thread

A single conversation. Roles flip freely — either person can ask next. No separate ask-thread vs DM.

keep it? · both opt in

Connection

Durable, mutual, in your network — easy to reach again. The deliberate "this one's worth keeping."

No cold DMs. You can't free-message a stranger — every thread starts from a consented opener (an ask or a hello). After acceptance, it's a normal conversation.

Thread vs Connection

Thread

the conversation
  • Opened by an ask or a hello.
  • Lives in your Inbox; roles flip freely.
  • If nothing more happens, it can quietly fade — no obligation.

Connection

the relationship
  • A thread you both chose to keep.
  • Durable, mutual, shows in your network.
  • Helping once never forces it — you opt in.

In one line: a thread is a conversation; a connection is a thread you both chose to keep — like texting someone vs. saving their number.

How an admin uses it

operator of the flywheel — not a CRM

The admin's job isn't to manage members; it's to keep the circle alive and activate it. They sit on top of the school's existing system of record, never replace it.

A1

Bring people in

· grow the circle
Phase 4Import roster from existing CRM / connector.
InviteSingle, or CSV bulk.
PendingNew sign-ups arrive for review.
ContextSee year, role, school, invite match.
DecideApprove or decline.
Wedge:Pull the roster in, write fresh consented data back — the school never rips out Raiser's Edge / Salesforce. Near-zero switching cost.
A2

Find anyone instantly

· the gap that blocks everything else today
SearchName, employer, city, year, freshness.
Filter / sortBy completeness, staleness, availability.
OpenOne member's full record.
ActMessage, correct, deactivate.
Today:There is no admin search at all — a 500-row dump. This is the single most-felt gap.
A3

Keep the data alive

· the moat
FreshnessWho's stale · last confirmed · pending proposals · cohort health.
NudgeBulk refresh a cohort.
BackgroundEnrichment sweep runs monthly (LinkdAPI · Bright Data · PDL).
Weekly digest"14 updated · 3 need you" — batched, never per-change.
CorrectSuggest or edit — with audit + member notice. Never silent.
Why we win:CRMs rot because nobody wants to be in them. Our data stays fresh because members want to be in it — an engine tops it up, and the admin hears about it in one weekly digest, not a stream of pings.
A4

Reach alumni

· the return engine
Post eventCapacity & waitlist handled.
Post newsAnnouncement, optional email fan-out.
CampaignOne-click templates to all or a segment — e.g. annual "Is your info still right?"
FeedbackRead rate · RSVPs · attendance · confirmations.
EffectPulls alumni back — and tops up the data.
Closes the loop:A one-click "confirm your info" campaign is comms that directly feeds freshness (A3) — engagement and living data from the same gentle touch. Today engagement is unmeasured; instrumenting it is what lets an admin learn what works.
A5

Understand & activate

· steer the network
DashboardSignups · activity · help req/response · freshness · RSVPs.
SpotA good connection worth making.
BridgeNudge it, or open a temporary cross-circle bridge — admin-led.
Guardrail:Search + bulk actions look CRM-ish. The "activation layer, not a CRM" line is what keeps this from drifting into the loss condition.

"Better than a CRM" = living data, not more features

We don't out-feature Salesforce — that's unwinnable and off-brand. We win the one axis incumbents can't: consented, self-maintaining data and member-first warmth, sitting on top of the system of record they already own. Even the simplest tool serves that — a one-click annual "confirm your info" campaign keeps the record alive without anyone touching a CRM.
