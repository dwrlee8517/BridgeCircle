Warm — a softer take on Civic Editorial

This is an alternate visual direction for BridgeCircle, derived from a universal-priority UI/UX evaluation. It keeps Electric Sky as primary and Inter as the family, but introduces a contrasting amber CTA, larger radius, a modular type scale anchored at 16px, and a collapsed accent palette. Use it for the A/B comparison in compare/; do not adopt without testing.

What's different from Civic

The warm variant is one CSS class (.theme-warm) overriding production tokens. Everything else inherits from app/src/app/globals.css.

Note

Primary color (Electric Sky), text (Obsidian), background (Platinum Bone), and font family (Inter / Inter Tight) are unchanged. The experiment isolates shape and CTA-color decisions from brand-identity decisions.

Color

Two foundational colors (Electric Sky + Amber CTA), one structural color (Obsidian on Platinum Bone), and two semantic accents (Sage = open, Rust = declined). Plum and Ochre, which carried weaker semantic load in Civic, fold into muted and amber respectively.

Collapsed from Civic

Civic had four accents (ochre, rust, sage, plum). Ochre rolls into the new amber CTA. Plum rolls into muted-foreground. The system goes from 4 accents to 2, with the amber doing the work plum and ochre used to do.

Typography

Inter family throughout. The scale moves to a 1.25 modular ratio anchored at body 16px, giving a predictable hierarchy from page heroes down to metadata.

vs Civic

Civic body is 13px, h1 is 20px, caption is 11px. The warm variant moves body up by 3px (the largest single change), h1 by ~6px, and caption by 2px. Smaller surfaces (dense admin tables) may still want the original Civic scale — this larger scale is for member-facing surfaces.

Cards

Cards are decision surfaces. In warm, interactive cards lift 2px and gain shadow on hover (vs Civic's border-only lift) — signals "you can click this" without breaking the editorial calm.

Density modes

Theme and density are orthogonal axes. A surface combines a theme (theme-warm) with a density (default or density-pro). The theme carries brand identity; the density carries surface job.

The two-axis model

Axis Controls Values
Theme (brand) Radius, palette, font family, CTA color civic · theme-warm
Density (job) Type sizes, padding, shadow weight, avatar size default · density-cozy · density-pro

Why orthogonal

Mixing brand identity across pages (different radius, different color) fragments the product. Mixing density across pages (same radius, smaller type) signals "this surface is doing different work" while keeping the brand anchor. LinkedIn, Notion, and Linear all do this — feed/page/issue surfaces are roomy, admin tables are dense, but the visual identity is constant.

Cozy keeps amber; pro drops it

Cozy and pro share font sizes, but cozy is for member-facing list surfaces (home with several match cards) and pro is for operator surfaces (admin tables). Member surfaces keep the amber CTA + warm hover lift because that's the brand promise to the member. Operator surfaces drop both: they have many equal-weight actions per row (Edit, Deactivate, Approve, Promote) and amber would over-claim.

What stays consistent across density

Brand identity tokens never flip with density. They flip only with theme.

  • --radius — 10px in warm regardless of density
  • Color palette — same blue, same accents
  • Font family — Inter / Inter Tight
  • Focus styles — same focus ring everywhere

About older alums

Pro density is for operator workflow, not "professional vibe in general." Member-facing surfaces used by older alums (45+) stay at default density even when they're serious content — declining near-vision after 40 means larger type matters more, not less.
