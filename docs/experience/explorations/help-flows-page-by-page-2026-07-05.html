Journey A · the asker

Asking for help

Search-first (Richard, 2026-07-05): the member types the question, sees who could answer it and why, and only then commits — to one person (the composer drafts a note tailored to both) or to the circle (reach and anonymity live on that branch). The question is typed once and travels everywhere. After send, the ask lives in exactly two places: its status view and, once accepted, Messages.

The five-slot rule Decided 2026-07-06

A member can hold five open asks at once — direct or circle. The sixth waits until a slot frees. Slots return when an ask ends: resolved, retracted, declined by every recipient, or closed at day 14. With declines now visible notes, a slot freeing on decline leaks nothing — the note itself is the event.

Buffer check, asker side

The asker sees who could answer, and why, before writing a word · search results are private — a member never learns they appeared and weren't asked · a no arrives as one kind, cushioned note, never silence and never a bare rejection · no read states, no timelines · the asker holds the accept on every offer · and the private tier is the open-ask default. Asking never costs face.

Decision record

Decided in review — Richard, 2026-07-06

Every open call from v1–v2 is now resolved. This is the record the screens get designed against; the two seams flagged at the end are the only things left to watch in the pilot.

Decided
Reach is immutable once published

No edits mid-flight — not audience, not tier. The asker can retract anytime (everything vanishes quietly, the slot frees) or let the 14-day close run. Widening happens only through the post-close "Open it to the circle" — a new ask, not an edit.

Decided
Five open asks at once

The anti-spam valve is a total cap, not a per-ask recipient limit. Slots return when an ask ends — resolved, retracted, declined by every recipient, or closed at day 14.

Decided
Recipients: asker's choice

One or several people per direct ask — the five-slot rule carries the abuse case. One recipient keeps the fully tailored note; several stay personal but recipient-neutral.

Decided
Anonymity, per open ask, in v1

Each open ask chooses anonymous or named at post time. Anonymous = revealed only to the helper whose offer is accepted; evidence lines derive from the ask's text only.

Decided
Give status: no clock, three strikes

"Open to helping" never expires on a timer. Three direct asks left to time out unanswered → quiet auto-pause until the helper returns. Any response, accept or decline, resets the count.

Decided
Topics: five, editable

Up to 5 standing topic chips on Give, add/remove anytime. They drive the AI-suggestion arm and Home's "Needs you this week."

Decided
Messages everywhere have a default

Offer notes, accept openers, and decline notes all come as a default line or custom text, AI-helped either way. Declines always carry one — there is no silent pass (next card).

Decided
No quiet pass — every decline carries a note

Declining, on either side, sends a cushioned note: common default reasons ("I can't take this on right now", "This one's outside what I can speak to"), custom text, or an AI-shaped line. Supersedes ADR 0011 · D5's invisible pass — record the divergence in the ADR when implementation starts.

Decided
Notification popover ships v1

The bell surfaces the moments neither side may miss — ask received, offer received, accepted — each shown once. After that they live only in the notification section and Messages; the popover is a tap on the shoulder, not a second inbox.

Decided
Smaller calls

Outcome note on resolve: yes — one optional, skippable line. Default Help mode: remember last, Get for first-timers. Weekly digest: not yet — transactional only. global search / ⌘K: removed entirely (search lives per-surface).

Help and People both stay

Split by query shape: People takes person-shaped queries ("people who graduated from the West Coast", "working in tech", "with a graduate degree") → profile, Connect, Message. Help takes need-shaped ones ("I'm trying to move from marketing to HR") → evidence, Ask, and the circle door.

The two seams to watch

1 · Decline copy is the buffer now. With invisible passes gone, the default reasons carry the whole two-sided mechanism: they must stay no-fault and unranked ("can't right now," never graded excuses), and the AI must cushion custom notes toward kindness. Watch the pilot for whether visible declines make hesitant members ask less — that fear is why the quiet pass existed. If asking drops, revisit.

2 · The ADR drift. This supersedes ADR 0011 · D5 (the invisible pass) and narrows the uniform-close mechanic to silence only. Update the ADR in the same change that implements declines, per the code-wins-fix-the-docs rule.
