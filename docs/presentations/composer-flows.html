2Coaching composer ~1 week

A guided form replaces the blank canvas

Three short steps replace the textarea-first form. The composer becomes the help. The user states what they're working on, picks the kind of help, then sees what the AI noticed about the helper before reading the draft. Defensible against incumbents who just bolted a button on a textarea.

What changes under the hood

• 3-step wizard replaces the single-screen form

• Structured intent (working on / kind of help) feeds prompt directly

• Pre-draft "signals" panel shows what AI will use

• Variant API: shorter / more direct / warmer (lens-based regen)

• Send still produces the same asks row; data shape unchanged

Tradeoffs

The form is the help. Aligns with "lower the barrier" thesis.

Transparency builds trust — user can see and edit signals.

Hard to copy: a competitor needs to rebuild the wizard, not paste a button.

More screens = more drop-off risk on mobile.

A user who just wants to fire off a quick question may resent the steps.

3Conversational composer ~1.5–2 weeks · real bet

Two-turn dialogue, then draft

The composer is a chat. The asker types one line; the AI reads both profiles and asks one focused question back; the asker answers; the AI surfaces the signals and drafts. Most magical. Most ambitious. Probably needs Sonnet, not Haiku, for the back-and-forth to feel intelligent.

What changes under the hood

• Composer is a chat surface, not a form

• Two-turn loop (clarify → confirm signals → draft)

• Likely Sonnet for clarification turn; Haiku still fine for variants

• Server-side state for the conversation (in-memory or short-lived row)

• Send still creates the same asks row at the end

Tradeoffs

Magical. The AI feels like a co-author, not a button.

Hardest for a copycat to ship — needs prompt + product muscle.

Handles ambiguous intent ("I dunno, just want advice") gracefully.

A chat for what could be a 30-second action may feel heavy.

Cost-per-draft rises (more turns, larger model).

Failure modes are louder — a bad clarifying question is more memorable than a bad draft.

★Hybrid (recommended) Chosen 2026-05-07

Coaching wizard + skip-path + signals-transparency from Option 3

Option 2's coaching wizard for first-time / unsure users, plus a "skip the wizard" affordance for return users who already know what to say, plus Option 3's signals-transparency moment shown before drafting in both paths. Two surfaces, one composer — same shape Gmail uses for Smart Compose vs. Help Me Write. Rationale lives in composer-ux-research.md.

Why this shape

• Lavender (sales) precedent: 20.5% reply, ~4× industry — coaching beat one-click

• Hinge / Bumble warning: AI homogenization corrodes warm-network trust

• Gmail precedent: two surfaces (Smart Compose + Help Me Write) for two intents

• Apple Writing Tools precedent: variants beat chat for known-text editing

Trade-offs accepted

First-time askers get coached; return askers get speed.

Signals panel = brand-side defense against "obvious AI" recipient impression.

More surface area than Option 1 — worth it given the rare/social/unclear-intent shape.

Skip path adds a second flow to maintain — keep prompt + draft service shared.
