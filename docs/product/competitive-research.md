# BridgeCircle Competitive Research

## Purpose

Snapshot of the alumni-network competitive landscape, prepared for the Chadwick pilot launch. Captures Gravyty in depth (the most aggressive consolidator in the category) and lighter scans of the other named competitors plus a few under-radar entrants.

The actionable distillation lives in `differentiation.md`. Read that for the "what we should actually do" — this document is the source material it draws on.

## 1. Gravyty Deep Dive

### What Gravyty actually is in 2026

Gravyty is no longer just "Graduway with AI bolted on." Over 2024–2025 it consolidated into a multi-product advancement-tech holding company:

- **Merger with Ivy.ai and Ocelot (2025)** — combined entity reports 150+ employees, 2,700+ education/nonprofit orgs, ~25M students/alumni ([source](https://gravyty.com/blog/gravyty-ivy-ai-and-ocelot-inc-5000/)).
- **Acquisition of Athlete Network (June 2025)** for collegiate athletics — Penn State, Texas Tech, Alabama on the customer list ([source](https://www.prweb.com/releases/gravyty-acquires-athlete-network-expanding-fundraising-and-engagement-capabilities-for-higher-education-athletic-departments-302486739.html)).
- Graduway is positioned as the **community + mentoring** layer; fundraising AI and chatbot/SMS tools sit alongside it.

The company is PE-owned (K1 Investment Management). PE-owned vendors typically raise prices, harden contracts, and prioritize enterprise upsell over SMB usability — a recurring pattern that opens specific gaps for new entrants.

### Mentoring features

Two named modes ([alumni mentoring page](https://gravyty.com/products/alumni-mentoring-platform/)):

- **Flash mentoring** — short, ad-hoc Q&A. Member posts a question, system surfaces willing mentors. Office-hours-on-demand.
- **Structured mentoring** — admin-defined cohorts with pre-defined matching criteria, automated matching, scheduled milestones, in-app live chat, admin reporting.

How it feels: structured mentoring is heavily admin-led (admins define programs, cohorts, application forms, matching rules, reports). The marketing verbs are "match, manage, scale" — admin verbs. Flash mentoring is more member-led but treated as the secondary motion.

### Rest of the product

- **Directory / Profiles** — searchable with filters. Reviewers note **city-name matching rather than zip/radius**, and filtering is clunky for cross-region searches.
- **Groups** — interest/affinity/class-year groups, admin-creatable.
- **Job & internship board.**
- **Events** — RSVP, ticketing, virtual events, CRM sync.
- **Messaging** — DMs + admin bulk email + Gratavid (integrated video messaging).
- **Fundraising tie-in** — deep integration with Raiser's Edge NXT and Salesforce.
- **Mobile** — native app exists; **map and "nearby alumni" features are mobile-only**, suggesting fragmented feature parity.

### Who buys it

Higher ed and nonprofits dominate. Customers: UCLA, Oxford, Wisconsin, Arizona, Tulane, Rice, Toronto, Helsinki, Aston, York/Schulich. Athletics (post-Athlete-Network): Penn State, Texas Tech, Alabama. Reported scale: 2,700+ orgs across the merged Gravyty entity. **K-12 presence is thin** — Almabase and ToucanTech are stronger here. Directly relevant to Chadwick.

### Pricing

- Custom-quote only; no published tiers, no free trial.
- Reviewers consistently call it expensive ("high annual fee," "less functionality at higher cost").
- Industry chatter pegs Graduway at **$15K–$50K+/year** for mid-sized institutions — flag as estimate, founder should pin down with a quote.
- Closest published anchor: Hivebrite Connect plan starts ~$799/mo (~$9.6K/year).

### Recent news (last 12–18 months)

- **2025 Inc. 5000** (third year).
- **Ivy.ai + Ocelot merger** (2025).
- **Athlete Network acquisition** (June 2025).
- AI fundraiser-enablement is the marketed headline; mentoring positioned as a community-side companion.

### What reviewers complain about

From [G2](https://www.g2.com/products/graduway/reviews), [Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/134140/Graduway/reviews/), [Software Advice](https://www.softwareadvice.com/nonprofit/graduway-profile/):

- "Less functionality than competitors at higher cost."
- Reporting is "beyond bare bones" — no email open rates, weak engagement analytics.
- Directory geography is broken — city-name matching, no zip/radius. Notification scoping is global (Boston alum gets LA event spam).
- Customer support time-zone gaps; "no, we can't do that" answers.
- Limited customization (no post categories, no video on news feed).
- Mobile/desktop feature mismatch.
- Long onboarding / setup.

The pattern: **Graduway is a competent enterprise tool that is admin-heavy, feature-uneven, and overpriced for what reviewers actually use.**

## 2. Competitor Landscape

| Vendor | One-line | Primary buyer | Mentoring strength | Member- vs admin-led | Notable vs Gravyty |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **PeopleGrove** | Career-access platform | Higher ed | Core feature — probably deepest mentoring product in the field | Mixed | Stronger student-facing UX; PE-acquired with reported mission drift |
| **Hivebrite** | General private community platform | Universities, corporates, nonprofits | Mentoring is a checkbox, not the wedge | Admin-led, very configurable, heavy | Broader feature set; slow onboarding; ~$799/mo entry |
| **Almabase** | Alumni engagement + fundraising | Strongest K-12 footprint | Built around giving + events, not mentoring | Admin-led | Better K-12 fit than Graduway; weaker mentoring; search inconsistencies |
| **ToucanTech** | All-in-one CRM + portal + website + email | Schools (UK-heavy K-12 + indie schools) | Mentorship matching present, not headline | Admin-led but lighter | Strong "single tool" pitch; less of a true mentoring product |
| **EnterpriseAlumni** | Corporate alumni platform | Fortune 500 (powers LinkedIn's own alumni network) | Networking + boomerang-hire focus | Admin-led with strong member self-serve | Wrong segment for Chadwick |
| **PeoplePath** | Corporate alumni + GDPR-grade compliance | Corporate (SAP marquee) | Light mentoring | Admin-led | Wrong segment for Chadwick |
| **Hoopstr** (Vaave rebrand, March 2026) | "Activate Your Alumni Capital" — AI-powered portal | Schools + corporates, India/global | Mentorship matching is a core pillar | Admin-led | Recent rebrand; aggressive on AI-profile-automation pitch |

### Newer / member-first entrants

- **Protopia** ([protopia.co](https://protopia.co/)) — closest philosophical neighbor to BridgeCircle. AI routes member questions to willing alumni via email, Slack, SMS rather than forcing platform login. Mentor identity stays hidden until accept. Customers: Duke, Denison, App State, Pitt. The founder should look at this carefully — Protopia has independently arrived at the "low-friction asking" thesis. Counter-positioning: Protopia is intentionally not a directory/profile/portal product, so BridgeCircle's broader stack is a real differentiator.
- **Wisr** (EAB-acquired) — student engagement, alumni-mentor-on-the-side. Less of a true alumni-network product.
- **MentorcliQ** — enterprise mentoring software, not alumni-adjacent.
- **Peerz, Mentory, 360Alumni** — minimal signal.

## 3. Where The Incumbents Are Weakest

Cross-cutting themes from the reviews and product surfaces:

1. **Admin-led design language.** Every product page leads with "match, manage, scale" verbs. Members are an audience, not the customer.
2. **Time-to-value is brutal.** Hivebrite and Graduway both have "long setup" complaints. Industry stat (cited by Gravyty itself): **62% of alumni say their network provides "little to no career value."**
3. **Search is universally weak.** Graduway uses city-name matching. Almabase reviewers cite inconsistent search. None offer confident natural-language search.
4. **Pricing is opaque and high.** No published tiers, no self-serve trial, no transparent SMB/K-12 entry. K-12 schools without enterprise budgets — exactly Chadwick — are underserved.
5. **Mobile/web feature parity is uneven.**
6. **"Friction to ask" is high.** Log in → find profile → request → wait → schedule. Protopia's existence is direct proof that lowering this friction is a real wedge.
7. **The platforms feel dead.** Once a directory has been browsed, there's no reason to return.
8. **Customer support is bifurcated** at smaller customers.

## 4. Open Questions / Primary Research

Things the public web couldn't answer that the founder should validate directly:

1. **Demo Graduway and Almabase as Chadwick.** Get on a sales call. Time the demo. Note the verbs the salesperson uses. Take screenshots.
2. **Get a written quote** from Graduway and Almabase for Chadwick-sized orgs. Highest-value unknown.
3. **Demo Protopia.** Understand exactly how email/SMS routing works, mentor-acceptance rates.
4. **Talk to one Chadwick alum** who has used Graduway/Almabase/PeopleGrove elsewhere.
5. **Verify K-12 footprint claims** at Almabase.
6. **Confirm the Gravyty pricing band** ($15K–$50K/year is unverified).
7. **Check Hoopstr post-rebrand.** March 2026 — recent enough that US K-12 push may or may not be real.
8. **Understand Athlete Network's mentoring UX.** If it's good, Gravyty is closing the gap.
