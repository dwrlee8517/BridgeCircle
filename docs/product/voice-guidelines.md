# BridgeCircle Voice Guidelines

> If you're writing copy for any surface — product strings, emails, AI drafts, marketing, slides — read this first.

## 1. Voice in one line

Warm, specific, calm. Like a thoughtful alumni coordinator who knows the community, notices good connections, and helps people reach out without pushing.

## 2. The narrator

Picture one person speaking through every product surface: **a thoughtful alumni coordinator.** They've been around the community for a while. They know names, remember details, and notice when two people should meet. They're older than most members, calm, observant, not chatty. They never hype. They never sell. They say what's true plainly and offer a next step.

**What the coordinator would never say:**

- "Hey there! 👋"
- "Unlock premium features"
- "Don't miss out"
- "Engage with the community"
- "We've matched you with…"

**What the coordinator sounds like:**

- "Jane is in Brooklyn, works at Figma in product design, and is open to mentoring students. She might be a good person to reach out to."
- "You haven't heard back from Mark yet. Want to send a gentle reminder, or wait a few days?"
- "Quiet week. 3 new alumni joined and 1 mentor refreshed their profile."

When you're stuck on a line, read it aloud as if the coordinator were saying it across a coffee table. If it sounds off, it is.

## 3. The priority reader

When voice tradeoffs come up, default to writing for **the embarrassed asker**: someone hesitant to reach out, unsure they belong, worried about bothering people.

The test for any important line: *would this make someone afraid to ask feel slightly less afraid?* If not, rewrite.

This does not mean coddling. Mentors and admins need different registers (see §11 Contextual matrix). But the **default voice is calibrated for the most hesitant reader in the room** — because that's the reader who quietly walks away when the voice is wrong.

## 4. Canonical tagline

> **Together, build a warm community that provides real support.**

Use this on: hero copy, primary marketing pages, partner decks, about-page header.

## 5. The five voice principles

Each principle is paired with what it is *not* — that's where it earns its keep.

### 5.1 Warm, not chummy

Warm assumes the reader is already in the room. Chummy tries too hard to make them feel welcome.

- ✅ "Welcome back, Maren."
- ❌ "Hey there! 👋 Great to see you!"

### 5.2 Specific, not vague

Numbers, names, and places beat abstractions.

- ✅ "3 mentors in Brooklyn, open this month."
- ❌ "Find your perfect match."

### 5.3 Calm, not urgent

No countdowns. No "act now." No streaks, badges, or growth-hack ornament. Urgency breaks the trust contract.

- ✅ "Your last role is from 2024. Want to confirm it's still current?"
- ❌ "URGENT: Update your profile now!"

### 5.4 Honest, not promotional

If a list is empty, say so. If the AI is unsure, hedge. If we don't know, don't make it up.

- ✅ "No mentorship requests yet. When someone reaches out, they'll show up here."
- ❌ "Your inbox is bursting with possibilities!"

### 5.5 Human, not algorithmic

The product should sound like a person made the choice — even when an algorithm did. (See §10 for when to disclose the AI.)

- ✅ "Jane works in product design at Figma and mentors students. Could be a fit?"
- ❌ "AI-matched: 94% compatibility."

## 7. Format rules

- **Case.** Sentence case for buttons, headings, nav, and labels. "Send request" not "Send Request."
- **Numerals.** Digits for all numbers, including small ones. "3 mentors" not "three mentors." Exception: spell out at the start of a sentence ("Three new alumni joined this week").
- **Dates.** Weekday + month + day for human-scannable dates: "Tue, May 24." Full form for formal contexts: "Tuesday, May 24, 2026."
- **Times.** Lowercase am/pm, no space: "7:30pm." Always include timezone for events that may cross regions: "7:30pm ET."
- **Punctuation.**
  - No trailing period on buttons or single-line labels.
  - Periods on multi-sentence body copy.
  - Em-dashes are welcome for warmth ("Welcome back — Iris asked after you."). Use sparingly.
  - Avoid exclamation marks. Exception: genuine celebrations ("Welcome to BridgeCircle!").
  - Oxford comma, always.
- **Capitalization of product terms.**
  - "BridgeCircle" — one word, capital B and C, no space.
  - "Bridge Programs" — both capitalized when referring to the feature.
  - "Circles" — lowercase in body copy; capitalized when used as a product noun.
- **Emoji.** Not in product copy, notifications, or transactional emails. Marketing / community posts may use sparingly. Never use 👋, 🎉, ✨, 🚀 in any user-facing surface. _(Marketing emoji policy is an open question — see §17.)_

| Empty state title | 40 chars | |
| Empty state body | 160 chars | Reason + warm next step. |

## 9. Microcopy patterns

### Empty states

Name what's missing in plain language. Offer one warm next step.

- ❌ "No data available."
- ✅ "No mentorship requests yet. When someone reaches out, they'll show up here."

### Buttons / CTAs

Verb + object. Honest about what happens next.

- ❌ "Submit" / "Continue" / "Get Started"
- ✅ "Send request" / "Save profile" / "Confirm details"

### Notifications & nudges

Reason-first. Never demand.

- ❌ "Update your profile now!"
- ✅ "Your last role is from 2024. Want to confirm it's still current?"

### Errors

Plain. Blameless. Actionable. Never expose raw error codes.

| Class | Example |
| --- | --- |
| Validation | "We couldn't save that. The bio is longer than 500 characters — try trimming it." |
| Auth | "Your session expired. Sign in again to keep going." |
| Permission | "This circle is private. The admin would need to invite you in." |
| Network | "We couldn't reach the server. Check your connection and try again." |
| System | "Something went wrong on our side. We've logged it — please try again in a moment." |

### Confirmation modals (destructive)

Lead with what's about to happen, not the question.

- ❌ "Are you sure?"
- ✅ "This will withdraw your request to Jane. She won't be notified. Withdraw?"

### Decline copy (mentor declines a request)

This is the **single most trust-defining surface in the product.** Default to: low-friction for the mentor, dignified for the asker.

For the mentor, offer these options without requiring a reason:

- "Not a fit right now"
- "Too busy this month" (dampens requests to them for 30 days)
- "Suggest someone else" (optional, opens a small picker)

For the asker:

- ✅ "Jane isn't able to help with this right now. We'll keep looking — would you like to see other people who might be a fit?"
- ❌ "Your request was declined."

The mentor's reason, if given, is **never** surfaced to the asker.

## 10. AI voice

The AI is a quiet helper, not the main character. Two rules govern visibility.

### 10.1 Transparent on drafts

When the AI writes user-facing text (a draft message, a draft reply, a draft event description), **say so.** Frame it as a starting point, not a finished product.

- ✅ "Here's a draft to get you started — edit before sending."
- ✅ "Suggested message — feel free to rewrite."
- ❌ Silent insertion of AI text into a Send field with no label.

Why: people will discover it's AI eventually. Disclosure now earns trust forever; concealment loses trust permanently.

### 10.2 Invisible on ranking

When the AI ranks, filters, or sorts (suggested people, suggested events, ordering of search results), **do not narrate the algorithm.**

- ✅ "Suggested for you" / "People near you" / "Recent activity"
- ❌ "AI-ranked by 94% compatibility" / "Our matching algorithm suggests…"

Why: ranking is plumbing. Calling attention to it makes the product feel like an AI demo. The result speaks for itself.

### 10.3 AI-drafted message style

When the AI drafts a message on behalf of a member, it should sound like:

- **Length.** 3–5 sentences. Never more than 600 characters.
- **Opening.** First name, no "Hi there." Default: "Hi Jane,"
- **Specificity.** Reference one concrete reason the reader is being contacted ("I saw you work in product design at Figma").
- **The ask.** One sentence, bounded in time ("Could I have 20 minutes to ask about your career path?").
- **Close.** "Thank you for considering," or "Either way — thanks for being part of the network." Never "Best regards" or "Looking forward."
- **Signature.** First name only. No title, no signature block.
- **Contractions.** Use them. "I'm," not "I am." "Can't," not "cannot."
- **Hedging.** Allowed when honest ("If this isn't a fit, no worries"). Forbidden when manipulative ("Just a small ask…").
- **Attribution.** The AI never speaks in first person plural ("we suggest"). It drafts on behalf of the member; the member is the author.

### 10.4 When the AI is uncertain

If the system isn't confident in a suggestion, say so plainly.

- ✅ "We don't have many alumni in your field yet — these three are the closest we found."
- ❌ Showing weak matches with no caveat.

## 11. Contextual matrix

Default voice is calibrated for members (especially the embarrassed asker). Two contexts modulate the default.

### 11.1 Admin dashboards & insights

Admins want signal, not warmth. The voice gets **drier, denser, more directly actionable** — but never corporate.

- **Keep:** specificity, honest empties, no jargon.
- **Adjust:** shorter sentences, more numbers, less hand-holding, no "welcome back" greetings.

Examples:

| Member voice | Admin voice |
| --- | --- |
| "Your last role is from 2024. Want to confirm it's still current?" | "42 alumni have profiles older than 18 months. Run a freshness campaign?" |
| "A few alumni near you joined this month." | "Bay Area: 7 joins, 3 mentor activations, 1 new cluster (biotech) crossed the meetup threshold." |
| "No mentorship requests yet." | "0 requests in past 14 days. Cohort response rate trending below 12%." |

Admin copy may use a small additional vocabulary: "cohort," "cluster," "activation rate," "freshness rate," "threshold." **These are admin-only.** Never use them in member-facing surfaces.

### 11.2 Sponsor / partner-hosted opportunities

Anything sourced from a partner organization (company-hosted session, sponsored event, partner-paid program) must be **clearly labeled and opportunity-framed, never ad-framed.**

**Required labeling, on every partner-sourced item:**

- "Hosted by [partner name]" — visible at the same scan-glance as the title.
- "Approved by [admin / org name]" — visible so members see the admin chain of trust.

**Tone rules:**

- Frame as **opportunity**, not promotion. "Figma is hosting a product careers night for alumni in the Bay Area" — not "Don't miss Figma's exclusive event!"
- Members opt in. They are never opted in by default.
- "Apply," "RSVP," "Express interest" — never "Claim your spot."

**Example:**

> **Bay Area product careers night**
> Hosted by Figma · Approved by Chadwick Alumni
>
> Open to alumni and current students in product design, product management, or engineering. 25 spots. Tue, June 16, 6:30pm PT, San Francisco.
>
> [Express interest]

**What's forbidden in partner copy:**

- Urgency framing ("Spots filling fast!")
- Exclusivity framing ("VIP access," "Exclusive opportunity")
- Vague benefit framing ("Network with industry leaders")
- Any visual treatment that hides the partner attribution

## 12. Copy cookbook — 10 worked examples

Full copy, not word swaps. Use these as starting points; do not paste verbatim.

### 12.1 Invitation email (school → new alum)

> **Subject:** You're invited to the Chadwick alumni circle
>
> Hi Maren,
>
> I'm reaching out from the Chadwick alumni office. We've built a place for alumni to find and support each other — mentorship, referrals, advice, the occasional dinner — and I'd like to invite you in.
>
> It's invite-only, free, and you choose what you share.
>
> [Accept invitation]
>
> If now isn't a good time, no need to respond — the invite stays open.
>
> — Iris Chen, Chadwick Alumni Office

### 12.2 First-time member empty state (no requests yet)

> **No mentorship requests yet.**
>
> When someone in the circle reaches out for help, they'll show up here. In the meantime, [browse alumni near you] or [add what you can help with] so the right people can find you.

### 12.3 AI-drafted first message (member → mentor)

> *Here's a draft to get you started — edit before sending.*
>
> Hi Jane,
>
> I'm a Chadwick '22 grad working in product design at a small startup in Brooklyn. I saw you've been at Figma for a few years and led the Config event team — I'd love to hear how you think about moving from IC work into bigger cross-team programs.
>
> Could I have 20 minutes sometime in the next two weeks? Happy to work around your schedule.
>
> Either way — thanks for being part of the network.
>
> — Maren

### 12.4 Mentor decline (member-facing)

> Jane isn't able to help with this right now. We'll keep looking — [see other people who might be a fit].

### 12.5 Profile freshness nudge

> Your last role on your profile is from 2024. Want to confirm it's still current, or [update it]?

### 12.6 Admin insight card

> **Bay Area · 1 new cluster**
>
> 42 alumni in the Bay Area · 18 in technology · 7 open mentors · 11 students interested in product or engineering.
>
> [Draft a Bay Area product careers night]

### 12.7 Confirmation modal (destructive)

> **Withdraw your request to Jane?**
>
> Jane won't be notified, and the request will be removed from her inbox.
>
> [Withdraw]   [Keep request]

### 12.8 Partner opportunity (in a feed)

> **Bay Area product careers night**
> Hosted by Figma · Approved by Chadwick Alumni · Tue, June 16, 6:30pm PT
>
> [Express interest]

### 12.9 Error toast (network)

> We couldn't reach the server. Check your connection and try again.

### 12.10 System status banner

> Some emails are sending slower than usual today. We're on it — your message will go out, just a few minutes behind.

### 12.11 Conditional RSVP — peer match notification

The "I'll go if…" feature ([spec](../specs/events-conditional-rsvp.md)) is where transparency-on-condition meets invisibility-on-algorithm. The notification names what the *member* wrote, never what the *system* matched.

> **A classmate just said the same.**
>
> Iris from the Class of 2022 said she'd go if classmates were there. So did you. Want to lock in your RSVP?
>
> [I'm in] [Not this time]

What this gets right:

- Names the matching member ("Iris from the Class of 2022"), not the algorithm
- States the member's own words ("she'd go if classmates were there") — that's the condition, not the system's interpretation
- Calm CTAs, no urgency, easy decline ("Not this time," not "Skip")
- No mention of "match," "compatibility," or "your condition was triggered"

### 12.12 Conditional RSVP — mutual deadlock notification

When two members each named the other as their condition, neither can resolve without the other committing first. The notification frames this as the social fact it is — *you're each waiting on each other* — not as a system event.

> **You're each waiting on the other.**
>
> You both said you'd go if the other was. Want to lock it in together?
>
> [I'm in] [Not this time]

What this gets right:

- The narrator notices a real social pattern and names it warmly
- No algorithm-speak ("mutual conditional dependency detected")
- "Lock it in together" affirms the shared decision — neither member is exposed as the hesitator

### 12.13 Ask mediator — coordinator approaches a helper on behalf of a member

The mediator outreach ([spec](../specs/ask-mediator.md)) is the strongest single example of the coordinator's institutional voice. The sender is BridgeCircle, the asker is named, the context is bounded, and the helper has a respectful out.

> **A Class of '22 alum is hoping you can help.**
>
> Maren ('22, Brooklyn) is looking for advice on moving from consulting to product. She specifically wanted to talk to someone who has done a similar move.
>
> Would you be open to a 20-minute chat with her?
>
> [I'm in]   [Not this time]   [Suggest someone else]

What this gets right:

- The sender is clearly institutional ("BridgeCircle here," via the email signature line) — no impersonation of the member
- The asker is named with peer context (class + city), not just "an alumna"
- The reason is specific ("moving from consulting to product") and member-supplied — not algorithm-narrated
- The ask is bounded ("20-minute chat") — voice §10.3 rule for AI-touched message length
- Three actions including "Not this time" — never "Decline," never "Reject," and the asker is never told who declined
- **"Not this time" goes to BridgeCircle, not to the asker — declining costs the helper nothing socially.** This is the mechanism by which the network stays warm: low-friction declines preserve the helper's willingness to be approached again. The buffer benefits both sides.
- No urgency, no exclamation, no "your perfect match has arrived"

### 12.14 Ask mediator — helper accepts, member is notified

When a helper says yes, the member is notified calmly. The notification names the helper but does not over-celebrate.

> **Jane said yes.**
>
> She's open to helping. Want to send your message?
>
> [Open draft] [Send later]

What this gets right:

- Two-word title carries the whole signal — no hype
- "Want to send your message?" returns agency to the member — BridgeCircle made the introduction; the member writes the conversation
- "Send later" is offered as a calm equal to "Open draft" — no FOMO

### 12.15 Ask mediator — search exhausted, no helper accepted

The hardest notification in the system. The member trusted BridgeCircle to ask on their behalf, and it didn't find a match. The voice has to be honest without being defeatist, and offer a real alternative.

> **We didn't find a match this time.**
>
> A few people weren't available. Want to send directly to someone instead, or wait a few more days?
>
> [Send directly] [Keep looking] [Save for later]

What this gets right:

- "Didn't find a match" is direct — no euphemism ("we're still working on it")
- "A few people weren't available" is honest — does not say *why* anyone declined, does not name them
- Three real options, none of which are "give up"
- No apology — apology would imply the system did something wrong; it didn't, the network simply didn't have a fit right now

## 13. Things the brand never does

- Streaks, leaderboards, gamification badges.
- "Unlock" / "premium" / "exclusive" / "VIP" framing.
- Push notifications without a stated reason.
- Surveillance-feeling features: who-viewed-your-profile, read receipts on every message, location tracking.
- Cold mass outreach. Spray-and-pray suggestions.
- Marketing fonts, neon gradients, oversized blob shapes, glow effects.
- AI-generated text on user-facing surfaces without disclosure.
- Partner content without "Hosted by" labeling.
- Korean copy via machine translation _(see §17 — Korean voice guide pending)_.

## 14. Wordmark & assets

- **Wordmark.** Fraunces (serif), display weight, slight negative tracking. "BridgeCircle" is one continuous word — never break it, never insert a space.
- **Pairing fonts.** Inter / Inter Tight for UI body and labels.
- **Color anchors.** Platinum Bone surfaces, Obsidian Ink structure, Cobalt action states. Full system in [`docs/experience/ui/design-system/`](../experience/ui/design-system/).
